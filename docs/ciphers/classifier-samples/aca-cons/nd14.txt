Dear KREWE,
It was March before makers of Minnesota
snow removal equipment finally had the
break they had been hoping for after running
double shifts all winter long. Luckily our
clearing operators were able and
determined, as was John Deere. The string
of snow storms this year created such a
workload for everyone out here that the
March break was badly needed all around,
not just for their employees.
It's especially important for those of us who
struggle with mountains of the white fluffy
stuff just about every day to get a break now
and then. Hard labor in cold temperatures
will inevitably damage backs. Even when
care is properly taken, your back is not
strong enough. The poor thing will with great
certainty have slipped discs or other issues
before long under such circumstances, as a
lot of people with chronic back problems at
the end of snow season can attest to out
here on the prairie.
We have the best boots, and beyond any
doubt also the most clever and advanced
removal equipment, but despite this we are
dealing with a truly miserable experience
when you have to keep at it practically every
day all winter long. Hope the next one will be
easier.
