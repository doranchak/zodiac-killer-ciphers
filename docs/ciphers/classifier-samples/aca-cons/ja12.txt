JA-1
At Utica the train was delayed for
longer than anticipated because of
one overheated bearing. The vast
crowd in the market square indulged
in a hearty testimonial addressed to
Mr. Lincoln but to which he excused
himself from speaking.

A salutation was had at Little Falls
for Mr. Lincoln. The town bells let out
a merry peel as the train stopped,
and the crowd excitedly called Mr.
Lincoln from the car to an impromptu
speech, but even though earnestly
sued to make a speech, he declined
entry on the platform for a formal
welcome.

Near Fonda people gathered about
to hail the President.
From Amsterdam to Schenectady,
yet at the train, an assembled
multitude gathered. What is more, as
the train approached the latter place,
someone, apparently so, shot a
cannon point blank onto the first
coach, smashing a door and
breaking glass windows. Then, some
were hit by broken glass but were
not injured.

In Albany the sounds of guns
announced the arrival at the station,
and Mr. Lincoln was met by Mayor
Thacher of Albany.
