Homophone cycles:

In the following image of the cipher text, all symbols that participate in unbroken homophone cycles are displayed in black. Symbols that do not follow a cycle pattern are displayed in gray. Cycles are considered after applying the transcription corrections analyzed above.

You can see how the cycle patterns mostly break down in Part 3 of the cipher. You can also see that the symbols intended to represent "L" often occur together, such as in the words "KILL", "THRILL", "WILL", "ALL", and "COLLECTING". If the killer had used predictable homophone cycles for "L", the meaning of those symbols would probably have been even more obvious. Nevertheless, the bigram still occurs six times, which was a great clue for the Hardens to follow.

Differences between Harden Key and Concerned Citizen Key:

  • In Concerned Citizen key, decodes to N and W. In Harden solution, decodes only to W. Perhaps author of Concerned Citizen key mistook for , which decodes to N.
  • In Concerned Citizen key, decodes to L and T. In Harden solution, decodes only to T. It's not clear why Concerned Citizen key author included L.
  • In Harden solution, decodes to A and S. In the Concerned Citizen key, decodes only to S.
  • In Harden solution, decodes to F and decodes to M. In Concened Citizen key, these are combined into a single symbol, , which decodes to F, K, L and M. It is not clear why K and L are included in this mapping.
  • Concerned Citizen key mistakenly decodes as both F and X. The Harden solution indicates that X is mapped to symbol instead.
  • In the plaintext-to-cipher mapping above, symbols are presented in the order in which they appear in the cipher text. The same is true of the left column of the Concerned Citizen key, which shows plaintext-to-cipher mappings. However, the Concerned Citizen key does not show the cipher symbols for L in the correct order.

Interpretation:

  • Qc7 is the portion of the 408 cipher accompanying the SF Examiner letter.
  • Qc11 is the portion of the 408 cipher accompanying the SF Chronicle letter.
  • Qc15 is the portion of the 408 cipher accompanying the Vallejo Times-Herald letter.
  • Q18 is the Concerned Citizen key.
  • FBI examination determined that key is "substantially accurate", and a "generally valid decryption; equivalents for Qc7, 11, and 15."
  • The first (left) column of the key maps plaintext letters to cipher symbols.
  • The second (right) column of the key maps cipher symbols to multiple plaintext letters.
  • In the left column, plaintext letter X is mistakenly mapped to instead of .
  • FBI interpreted as cipher symbol .
  • FBI could not verify K and L to be valid mappings for cipher symbol .
  • F should be mapped to .
  • FBI could not verify N to be valid mapping for cipher symbol .
  • FBI could not verify O to be valid mapping for cipher symbol . However, you can see in the Harden solution that this arises from the transcription errors in the word "DANGEROUS" (see [8]).
  • FBI could not verify L to be valid mapping for cipher symbol .
